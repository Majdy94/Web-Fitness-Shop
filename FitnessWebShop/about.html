Sport History

Calisthenics, free body exercises performed with varying degrees of intensity and rhythm,
which may or may not be done with light handheld apparatuses such as rings and wands. The exercises employ such motions as bending, stretching, twisting, swinging, kicking, and jumping,
as well as such specialized movements as push-ups, sit-ups, and chin-ups. Calisthenics promote strength,
endurance, flexibility, and coordination and augment the body’s general well-being by placing controllable, regular demands upon the cardiovascular system. The exercises can function as physique builders or serve as warm-ups for more-strenuous sports or exertions.
